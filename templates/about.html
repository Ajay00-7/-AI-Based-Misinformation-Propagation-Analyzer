About the Project & Models

Project Overview

The AI Misinformation Detection & Propagation Analysis System is a cutting-edge tool designed to identify fake news and analyze how it spreads across social networks. By combining advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) with epidemiological models, this system provides a comprehensive view of information authenticity and its potential impact.

BERT Model

We utilize Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT), a state-of-the-art language model developed by Google. BERT understands the context of words in search queries and text, allowing for highly accurate classification of news articles as "Real" or "Fake" based on semantic analysis rather than just keyword matching.

SVM Baseline

A Support Vector Machine (SVM) acts as our baseline model. While efficient and effective for text classification, it serves as a comparison point to demonstrate the superior performance of deep learning approaches like BERT in handling complex linguistic nuances.

SIR Propagation Model

To simulate how misinformation spreads, we adapt the Susceptible-Infected-Recovered (SIR) model from epidemiology.

  • Susceptible: Users who haven't seen the news.
  • Infected: Users sharing the fake news.
  • Recovered: Users who identified it as fake and stopped sharing.

Network Analysis

We simulate social networks to identify key influencers and spread patterns. Our 3D visualization tools allow you to explore how information flows through user nodes, helping to identify "Super Spreaders" and potential intervention points to stop viral misinformation.

Why It Matters

In an era of digital information overload, the ability to distinguish truth from fabrication is critical. This project aims to empower users with AI-driven insights, making the invisible mechanisms of misinformation visible and understandable.
